About Me

I am a Canadian Front-End Developer who grew up in Oshawa, Ontario and got my degree from Trent University. I spent over three years working for the University as a web developer under the External Relations & Advancement department. During my time at Trent I spent my time working with their Philanthrophy team developing front facing projects to better serve Trent's donors as well as working on the backend database. Due to my time working with the fundraising department I have extensive experience working with Blackbaud's non-profit software such as Raiser's Edge NXT database and their NetCommunity web platform. My time as a developer included leading a team of other students on the redesign of the alumni community website and working with stakeholders and communicating my teams progress on assigned projects.

Using my years of previous experience as a developer I am using my Front-End skills to build websites and web applications for small business and individuals in Canada as a freelance web developer. Using my skills I can help small businesses grow and access the potential that the web unlocks.

In my spare time I enjoy experimenting with new technologies such as Python, R, Data Analysis and Machine Learning and VR. I first became interested in computer science because of the book "Moneyball" by Micheal Lewis and it's introduction to advanced baseball analytics. It opened my eyes to a whole new world and lead me down the path to today. My dream is to one day work in a major league baseball operations deparment as a data analyst.
